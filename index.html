DSC 106 Final Project

NYC Public Transportation

Exploration of the details, history, and effectiveness of New York City's MTA public transportation.

By: Nikita, Irina, Natalie ·

The MTA's history in New York City is rooted in the developement of infrastructure that began in the 19th century. Despite decades of barriers, including bankrupty and operational failure, the New York City's MTA system is still the busiest rapid transit system in the Americas, carrying approximately 650 million riders annually. The following visualizations explore the history of the traffic of the MTA system, as well as recent key indicators of the effectiveness of the transit system today.

In order to create these visualizations, we used the following public datasets provided by the MTA: MTA Monthly Ridership, MTA Subway Hourly Ridership, MTA Pulse Survey Beginning 2025

Current Progress

As of now, we have started with the first part of our data storytelling which is comparing different types of public transit. For the "All" tab, the chart is stratified by transit mode and month, allowing the viewer to compare all modes for each corresponding year. While the viewer is given the ability to toggle between each category, the purpose of the all tab is to show that in every year across almost every month when subway system data starts being recorded, the subway has the highest amount of riders. This would be essentially in highlighting to show why so much money is being allocated for the 5 year MTA capital plan in comparison to all transit systems. Starting off with this plot allows for us to show the viewer why the subway system is the most essential form of transit, and later segue into larger takeaways. We also added a Peak Year button that jumps the slider to the highest ridership year for whichever agency the user selects. This makes it easy to compare how different agencies peaked at different times. We also added callouts that now appear automatically for notable years; for example, when a viewer selects 2020, a callout explains the COVID-19 ridership collapse and its scale.

Challenges

Some challenges include incorporating the storytelling of this project to give direct takeaways and ensuring that no information is miscommunicated. For example, even thought the nY Subway system was built in 1904, the data recorded from MTA only begins in 2018, while other systems like the B&T and MNR are recorded in 2008. This makes it seem like there is a large transition in 2018 where the NY subway system all of a sudden has the largest ridership, so a viewer not paying attention might assume that B&T has had the highest ridership in all the years even tho this might not be true. Other challenges would be making the visualizations more meaningful such as adding callouts for context (ridership would fall for all transit during the pandemic), doing research to add when certain policies were in place, and incorporating sentiment data that we have previously analyzed to show how policy actually affects riders in other ways than just raw numbers in participation. The most challenging thing will be designing a visualization which frames this information without being overwhelming which might entail adding animations or timed transitions.
