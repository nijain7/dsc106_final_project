NYC Public Transportation

Exploration of the details, history, and effectiveness of New York City's MTA public transportation.

By: Nikita, Irina, Natalie ·

The MTA's history in New York City is rooted in the developement of infrastructure that began in the 19th century. Despite decades of barriers, including bankrupty and operational failure, the New York City's MTA system is still the busiest rapid transit system in the Americas, carrying approximately 650 million riders annually. The following visualizations explore the history of the traffic of the MTA system, as well as recent key indicators of the effectiveness of the transit system today.

In order to create these visualizations, we used the following public datasets provided by the MTA: MTA Monthly Ridership, MTA Subway Hourly Ridership, MTA Pulse Survey Beginning 2025
